Rob Clark is a strategist, creator, author, and speaker who has built one of the most successful organic social media brands in the world. As the founder of That Tall Family, Rob and his family have generated over 5 billion views and built a following of more than 4 million people across Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Facebook, and LinkedIn without relying on paid ads.

His work sits at the intersection of attention, storytelling, leadership, and culture. Rob helps brands, universities, nonprofits, and entrepreneurs win attention and turn it into real growth — through consulting, content creation, digital development, and keynote speaking. His approach is practical, direct, and grounded in real world experience. Rob lives just south of Nashville, Tennessee.

What Rob Speaks About

Signature Keynote

5 Billion Views: Stop Being the Best Kept Secret

You’re great at what you do. Your clients love you. Your work speaks for itself. And yet somehow, the people who are less talented, less experienced, and less qualified are getting the attention, the clients, and the opportunities that should be yours.

That’s because the best don’t win. The known win.

Rob Clark built That Tall Family from a single 11 second video into a brand with over 5 billion organic views and more than 4 million followers across every major platform. In this talk, he shares the exact framework he uses to help businesses, universities, and professionals stop being invisible and start being the name everyone knows. No ad spend. No dance trends. No pretending to be someone you’re not. Just a proven system for turning what makes you different into the reason people choose you.

Your audience will walk away with:

  • The One of One framework for finding what makes them the only choice in their market
  • The hook strategy that turns two seconds of attention into lasting trust
  • The mindset shift that transforms social media from something they dread into the most powerful tool in their business

This talk is for any organization tired of being the best kept secret in their industry.
